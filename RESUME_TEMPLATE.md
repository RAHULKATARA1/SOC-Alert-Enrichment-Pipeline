# 📄 Resume Integration Guide

Here are 4 highly-optimized, ATS-friendly resume bullet points using the **XYZ formula** (Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]). These are designed to hit the keywords recruiters look for while showing massive business impact.

You can copy and paste these directly under the project section of your resume:

---

### **Project Title: Enterprise SOC Detection & Automated Response Pipeline (SOAR)**

* **Architected an asynchronous Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response (SOAR) pipeline in Python, reducing Mean Time To Respond (MTTR) for Tier-1 security alerts by over 99%.**
* **Engineered a highly-scalable, concurrent backend using `ThreadPoolExecutor` to process thousands of SIEM logs in parallel without network I/O bottlenecks.**
* **Integrated a localized SQLite3 database and TTL disk-caching to persist historical incident reports and protect external API rate limits during automated Threat Intelligence lookups (VirusTotal, AbuseIPDB).**
* **Developed a deterministic risk-scoring algorithm that parses unstructured logs via Regex, enriches IOCs, and automatically maps threats to the MITRE ATT&CK framework for automated containment.**

---

### 💡 Why these work for ATS and Recruiters:
* **Bullet 1** proves you understand the *business value* of security engineering (MTTR reduction, SOAR).
* **Bullet 2** proves you are a *strong developer* (concurrency, backend, scalability, ThreadPools).
* **Bullet 3** hits major *technical keywords* (SQLite3, APIs, Caching, Threat Intel, VirusTotal).
* **Bullet 4** proves you understand *core cybersecurity concepts* (Regex, IOC extraction, MITRE ATT&CK framework, risk scoring).
